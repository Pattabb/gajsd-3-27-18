The Buzz Word...."Data Visualization"

What comes to mind when you hear this word?

Charts * Graphs * Dashboards * Maps

How about the following?

Axes * Scales * Legends * Colors Palletes

Why is Data Visualization so important?

⇒ Graphics are memorable, so the message sticks.

⇒ Consuming large volumes of data is nearly impossible without visualization methods.

⇒ Visualization tells the story succinctly.

Intro To D3.js

D3 was the brainchild of Mike Bostock while working on his Ph.D at Stanford.

After being hired by The New York Times he brought D3 to mainstream america which included the following pieces:

⇒ How the US and OPEC Drive Oil Prices

⇒ Midterm Elections

⇒ Geography of Government Benefits

It's the most popular javascript Data Visualization library with many other building off it, such as:

DC.JS * NVD3.JS * D3.GEOMAP.JS * D3-LEGEND
Intro To D3.js - Continued

Why D3?

⇒ D3 allows you to bind arbitrary data to a Document Object Model (DOM), and then apply data-driven transformations to the document.

⇒ It incorporates the Enter\Update\Exit methodology.

⇒ Very detailed API Library.

⇒ Several Support Group(s) and Meetups

D3 Mapping Concepts

• Data...which comes in two forms:

⇒ GeoJSON & TopoJSON....both of which had thier beginings as shapefiles (.shp)

• Define a Projection - List of D3 Projections

⇒ Projection is the process of transforming Geographic lat\long coordinates into Cartesian pixel coordinates.

• Include a Path Generator - d3.geo.path()

⇒ Generators are responsible for connecting the dots and drawing the lines within an svg, which means we will need to include an svg as well.

• Import The Data Using A "Convenience Method" - d3.json()

⇒ Convenience methods provide a means to import data (csv,tsv,json,etc) and wait for it to be loaded before execting a callback function
