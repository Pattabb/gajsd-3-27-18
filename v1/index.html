Class Info

JavaScript Development (JSD) is comprised of 60 hours of in-class instruction spread evenly over 20 classes.

JSD breaks down into four units:

  1. Fundamentals of JavaScript
  2. The Browser and API's
  3. Persisting Data and Advanced Topics
  4. Building and Deploying Applications

In addition to weekly homework assignments, students are expected to complete three, standalone projects:

  1. Slackbot
  2. Feedr
  3. Final Project

Pre-Course Checklist

JSD assumes students possess a working knowledge of HTML and CSS.

As we will not be covering these two topics during the course, it is HIGHLY RECOMMENDED that students work through and complete General Assembly's Dash - an online tool that teaches basic HTML, CSS, and JavaScript through projects in the browser.

Additionally, students must complete the following before the start of class:

  1. Complete all of three of the lessons in CodeAcademy's JavaScript Fundamentals track.
  2. Complete CodeSchool's Try Git exercise. This will introduce you to git and GitHub, two technologies we'll be using to store our code and collaborate with others during the course.

A document outlining the pre-course checklist can be found here.

Graduation Requirements

In order to successfully complete JSD, students must:

  • Submit 80% of homework assignments
  • Attend 80% of classes
  • Successfully complete the course's final project

Communication

Communication between the instructional team and students is paramount for a successful learning environment. Students are strongly encouraged, at their convenience, to reach out to members of the instructional team with any questions.

Slack will serve as the primary tool for communication between students and the instructional team. Before the first class, you will receive an invitation to our Slack room:

https://jsd4.slack.com/

Slack can be accessed through the browser, or, through a native application that runs on your machine.

Homework

Homework will be assigned at the end of class every Thursday and is due the following Tuesday.

Files and materials for homework will be distributed during class, and are also available through this website.

Students will push (upload) homework to their respective Github repositories for feedback and grading.

Note: while late homeworks will be accepted, they will not receive feedback. Please schedule office hours if you would like feedback on late homework.
